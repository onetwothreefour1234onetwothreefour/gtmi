Pathway measures where the visa leads — the clarity and attainability of
progression to permanent residence and citizenship, and the tax treatment
during the validity period. At 22% of PAQ it carries the second-highest
pillar weight because mobility is rarely a one-step transaction; the
candidate is choosing a future, not just a current visa.

Three sub-factors. Permanent residence pathway (PR provision available,
minimum years of residence to PR, physical presence requirement during
accrual, PR retention rules); citizenship pathway (citizenship provision
available, total minimum years to citizenship, dual citizenship permitted,
civic and language test burden); tax treatment for new talent (residency
trigger in days per year, special-regime availability, territorial versus
worldwide taxation). High scorers offer a clearly published PR track at
five years or fewer with reasonable physical-presence requirements, allow
dual citizenship, and either tax territorially or run a credible new-talent
tax regime.
