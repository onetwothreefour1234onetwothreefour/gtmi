Stability measures how reliable the promise is — whether the rules a
candidate plans around today are likely to still be the rules in three or
five years. At 15% of PAQ it is the most proprietary pillar, because no
incumbent index measures policy reliability seriously, and it is the
clearest commercial differentiator GTMI offers.

Three sub-factors. Policy stability (count of material policy changes in
the last five years, whether forward-announced pipeline changes are
published, programme age capped at 20 years); transparency (published
approval-rate or admission statistics, published quota or cap information,
public guidance and decision-criteria quality); institutional quality (rule
of law and government effectiveness, sourced from external indices —
World Bank Worldwide Governance Indicators and V-Dem). High scorers publish
their numbers, announce changes ahead of time rather than effective
immediately, and operate inside countries that the standard governance
indices score well.
