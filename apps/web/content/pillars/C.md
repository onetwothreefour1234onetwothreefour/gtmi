Rights measures what the visa holder can actually do once granted. At 20%
of PAQ it sits in the middle of the pillar weight ranking — important
because the practical experience of holding the visa is what determines
whether candidates accept an offer and whether they stay long enough to
contribute meaningfully.

Three sub-factors. Labour-market flexibility (employer-sponsorship
requirement, ability to switch employers, self-employment and secondary
income rights, labour-market test); family rights (spouse inclusion and
work rights, dependent child age cap, parent or extended family inclusion,
same-sex partner recognition); access to public services (public
healthcare, public education for children). High scorers minimise tying the
visa to a single employer, automatically include the immediate family with
work rights, and grant access to public services on day one rather than
after a qualifying period.
