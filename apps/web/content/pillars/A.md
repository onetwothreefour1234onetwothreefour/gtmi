Access measures who can even apply. It carries the highest pillar weight in
PAQ — 28% — because the eligibility criteria are the single biggest
determinant of a programme&rsquo;s real-world utility. A programme that
scores well on every other pillar but admits only a narrow professional
slice serves few candidates; a programme with broad eligibility makes the
rest of the architecture genuinely usable.

The pillar combines three sub-factors: economic access (salary thresholds,
alternative qualification pathways), human capital requirements (minimum
education, work experience, language), and structural selectivity
(occupation list constraint, annual quota, age cap). High-scoring programmes
combine reasonable income or alternative routes, achievable academic and
language bars, and an open or transparently-published occupation list.
