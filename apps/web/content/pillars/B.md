Process measures how hard the application itself is — the friction the
candidate experiences between deciding to apply and receiving a decision.
At 15% of PAQ it is the lowest-weighted pillar, deliberately, because
process friction is recoverable: a slow programme can become fast, a paper
form can be digitised. The pillar still matters because process quality
predicts whether a candidate completes the application at all.

Three sub-factors. Time to decision (published SLA in days, fast-track
option, number of application steps); direct cost (principal applicant
fees, per-dependent fees, employer-borne levies, mandatory non-government
costs); digital and administrative access (online application availability,
in-person and biometric requirements, appeal-process clarity). High scorers
publish a binding SLA, run a digital-first pipeline, and minimise the
out-of-pocket cost the applicant carries personally.
